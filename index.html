About

The pandemic has brought out the best and worst in people. For me, this has included wearing the same chenille lounge pants for a week straight (very comfortable, highly recommend), worrying about the future, and learning to code.

I was one of those millennial kids who stayed up until 2AM on a school night perfecting my myspace layout, scrapping the original styles and creating a whole new web page. I didn't even realize it but I was learning basic HTML and CSS.

Over the past couple of years I had been thinking about really learning how to code but I always had excuses, not enough time being at the top of the list. Now I, like a lot of people, have found myself with an abundance of time. So I finally mustered up the courage, ignored the imposter syndrome, and learned the basics.

I was able to build a portfolio and land an internship creating a website for a startup company. A few months later I started my first full time job as a web developer creating and maintaining Wordpress sites for a variety of clients. Since then I've learned so much I can't even believe it. My skills have grown in PHP, editing Wordpress themes and plugins, using Google Analytics and Google Maps API, webhosting with Godaddy and Amazon Lightsail, SSH, PHPmyadmin, the list goes on. I think something that makes me stand out from other devs is that I am quite fantastic with clients and have a great service oriented attitude. I am also good at explaining technical concepts to clients.

I really enjoy web development and design because it requires a level of logic, problem solving, and creativity.

I live in Milwaukee, WI with my partner and two cats. I love to travel and travel often, no vacation days are wasted. I love trying new restaurants and exploring different cuisines. I like listening to lo-fi pop/rock, and going to concerts. I also enjoy cooking extravagant meals that take a whole day to prepare and sharing them with my family and friends.
